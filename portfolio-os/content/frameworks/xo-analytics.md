# X/O Analytics: Connecting Experience Data to Operations

Experience data (X) tells you how customers feel. Operational data (O) tells you what actually happened. Reported in isolation, neither answers the question that matters: what do we do differently?

I structured our analytics practice around 6 patterns that force X and O to intersect. Each pattern produces a different type of actionable insight.

## The 6 Patterns

**X Why** — *"Our NPS dropped. Why?"*
Use operational data to explain an experience signal. Find the process failure behind the score movement.

**O Drivers** — *"Our handle time spiked. What does that do to satisfaction?"*
Find the experience impact of an operational event. Understand the customer cost of operational decisions before making them.

**X&O Predict** — *"Where will satisfaction be in 90 days?"*
Build forward projections by combining trend lines from both data streams. Used for executive forecasting and board reporting.

**X&O Personalise** — *"This segment has high NPS but low renewal rate — what's different about how we treat them?"*
Adjust CS treatment based on combined signals. Prevents the trap of over-investing in happy customers while at-risk accounts go quiet.

**X&O Alert** — *"Flag any account where satisfaction drops while operational metrics look healthy."*
Proactive alerts that catch the accounts that would otherwise fall through the cracks — clients who are polite in surveys but disengaging in practice.

**X Value** — *"What was the revenue impact of the CX improvement programme?"*
Measure the business outcome of experience investments. The only pattern that speaks directly to the CFO conversation.

## Why this matters

CX programmes lose organisational credibility when metrics are reported in isolation. A 4.2 NPS score means nothing to a CFO. An X Value analysis showing that a 1-point NPS improvement correlates with a 12% reduction in churn rate — that gets budget approved.
