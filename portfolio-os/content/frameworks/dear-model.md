# DEAR Model: Predicting Churn 6 Months Early

Most SaaS teams find out a customer is at risk when the renewal conversation turns cold. By then it's too late to recover the relationship — you're negotiating from weakness.

I built the DEAR model to change that. It scores every account across four dimensions, updated monthly, and surfaces at-risk accounts 6 months before their renewal date.

## The 4 Dimensions

**D — Deployment**
Is the platform properly configured and live? Clients who never completed onboarding churn at 3x the rate of fully deployed accounts.

**E — Engagement**
Are users logging in? Are they returning? A drop in engagement is the earliest leading indicator of churn — often 9 months before renewal.

**A — Adoption**
Are the platform's workflows embedded in the client's daily operations, or is it a tool they log into for monthly reports?

**R — ROI**
Can the client articulate measurable value from the platform? If they cannot answer "what has this done for us?", they will not renew.

## What the data showed

Clients with healthy Deployment scores (D = High) had **15% higher gross renewal rates** than clients with identical Engagement and Adoption scores but weak Deployment. This was counterintuitive — we expected Engagement to be the strongest predictor. It turned out that clients who never finished setup were quietly resentful of the product.

## The result

Retention improved from **70% to 96%** over 18 months after the model was integrated into the CS workflow. The CS team shifted from reactive firefighting to proactive account management — working the flagged accounts 6 months out instead of scrambling at 30 days.
