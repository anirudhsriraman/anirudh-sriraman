# Government & Telecom CX at Scale

**Metrics:** 810K+ annual calls | 25% CSAT improvement | 30% faster service delivery

## Context

Two enterprise clients in regulated GCC markets needed to modernise their citizen and customer contact operations. TD123 was a Brunei government mandate to centralise national citizen services through a single contact centre, handling 810K+ calls annually with no existing omnichannel infrastructure. Imagine Telecom was a regional carrier with declining CSAT scores and slow service delivery cycles, losing ground to competitors on both resolution speed and upsell conversion.

## Decision

Rather than adapt a generic contact centre product, I led the decision to build tailored platform configurations for each client: a high-availability omnichannel layer for TD123 handling 810K+ annual calls, optimised for regulated-market compliance, and a workflow-driven service delivery model for Imagine Telecom focused on resolution speed and upsell trigger points. Both deployments shared a common feedback integration layer to enable continuous CSAT measurement without separate tooling overhead.

## Action

For TD123, led product development across platform architecture, workflow engine design, and omnichannel model configuration. Worked directly with Brunei government stakeholders to translate regulatory and operational requirements into a contact centre spec that achieved 95% of calls answered within 10 seconds and an 80%+ first call resolution rate. For Imagine Telecom, redesigned service delivery workflows to eliminate handoff delays and embedded upsell prompts at natural resolution points. Aligned both clients to a shared feedback integration spec to enable CSAT tracking at scale.

## Outcome

TD123 handled 810K+ annual calls with a 4.1/5 CSAT score, 95% answer rate within 10 seconds, and 80%+ first call resolution. Imagine Telecom saw a 25% improvement in CSAT, a 30% reduction in service delivery times, and a 20% increase in upsell success rate. Both deployments demonstrated that a single platform architecture, configured for context, could meet government-grade reliability requirements and commercial CX targets simultaneously.
