# Inheriting Engineering During the Great Resignation

**Metrics:** 40 engineers led | 0 penalties incurred | 3 PMs promoted

## Framing

A VP Product reading this sees org design executed under crisis: a PM career ladder built from the ground up, an ownership model that gave engineers direct product accountability, and cross-functional leadership that held a 40-person organisation together through sudden leadership loss. The zero-attrition outcome was not luck  -  it was the result of deliberate cultural architecture built during the transition.

## Context

The VP of Engineering departed suddenly. Eight senior engineers followed within weeks. A $2M backlog order was at risk. I stepped in to lead 40 engineers with no prior mandate to do so.

## Decision

Week-one: one-on-ones with every team lead. I was transparent about my limitations: "I'm not pretending to be a CTO. I'm here to unblock you."

## Action

Changed workflows to expand demo days for API teams, giving engineers direct visibility and ownership. Maintained the culture of psychological safety through the transition.

## Outcome

Zero attrition over 15 months against a 20%+ industry average during the same period. The $2M order was delivered on time with zero contract penalties. Three PMs were promoted during this stretch. The team's identity shifted from fear of the departure to pride in the delivery.
